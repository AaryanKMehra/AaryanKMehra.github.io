Butter Chicken

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 20 mins üç≥ Cook: 30 mins üë• Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 800g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 tomatoes, blended into puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, and salt for at least 2 hours.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp butter in a pan and cook the marinated chicken until done. Set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add remaining butter and saut√© onions until golden brown.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook for 5-7 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add cooked chicken back to the pan and mix well.
  6. Stir in heavy cream and garam masala, cooking for another 5 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan bread.

üí° Chef's Tip:

For best results, use chicken thighs instead of breast as they stay more tender and juicy. Add a pinch of sugar if the sauce seems too acidic.

Chicken Tikka

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 30 mins üç≥ Cook: 20 mins üë• Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 800g boneless chicken, cut into 1.5-inch cubes
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp coriander powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for grilling
  • Lemon wedges and onion slices for serving

Instructions:

  1. Mix yogurt with ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Add chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring all pieces are well coated.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight for best flavor.
  4. Preheat grill or grill pan to high temperature and lightly oil it.
  5. Thread chicken pieces onto skewers, leaving small gaps between pieces.
  6. Grill for 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally until cooked through with charred spots.
  7. Serve hot with lemon wedges, onion slices, and mint chutney.

üí° Chef's Tip:

If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes before grilling to prevent burning. For a smoky flavor, brush with oil and cook over charcoal.

Maggi (Noodles)

‚è±Ô∏è Prep: 5 mins üç≥ Cook: 8 mins üë• Serves: 1-2

Ingredients:

  • 1 packet Maggi noodles (80g)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp oil or butter
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Maggi masala seasoning pack (included)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or butter in a pan and add chopped onions, green chili, and garlic. Saut√© for 2 minutes.
  2. Add 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  3. Break the Maggi noodles and add them to the boiling water along with the seasoning pack.
  4. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. If adding egg, crack it into the pan and gently stir to cook it with the noodles.
  6. Add salt, black pepper, and lemon juice to taste.
  7. Cook for another 1-2 minutes until noodles are soft and broth is well combined.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot in a bowl.

üí° Chef's Tip:

Add vegetables like carrots, peas, and bell peppers for a healthier version. A drizzle of sesame oil at the end adds a nice Asian flavor twist!

Ingredients for Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (or 1 can)
  • 3 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp amchur (dried mango powder)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Fresh cilantro and green chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Making Bhature: Mix flour with yogurt, salt, baking soda, asafoetida, and sugar. Add warm water gradually to form a soft dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Divide dough into equal balls and roll into 3-inch thick discs.
  3. Heat oil for deep frying and fry each bhature until puffed and golden brown on both sides.
  4. Making Chole Curry: Heat oil and saut√© onions until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  6. Add tomatoes and cook until they become soft and blend into the oil.
  7. Add cumin, coriander, garam masala, amchur, and chili powder. Stir well.
  8. Add cooked chickpeas and 1 cup water. Let simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Taste and adjust salt and spices as needed.
  10. Serve hot bhature alongside the warm chole curry, garnished with cilantro and green chili.

üí° Chef's Tip:

For softer bhature, add a pinch of baking powder along with baking soda. Serve with fresh lime pickle for an authentic taste. If chickpeas are canned, drain and rinse them before using.
